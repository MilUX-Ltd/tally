# Lobster Robotics

> Dutch developer of autonomous underwater drones that produce high-resolution optical maps of the seabed.

## What they do

Lobster Robotics designs, manufactures and operates fully integrated autonomous underwater drones. A TU Delft spin-out based at RoboHouse in Delft, the company specialises in automated subsea photogrammetry, combining large numbers of seabed photographs into a single high-resolution, georeferenced optical map, an approach distinct from sonar-based survey. It both sells and rents its platforms and runs them as a survey and inspection service for offshore wind, cable and pipeline, scientific and defence customers.

## Products and solutions

- **Scout** - autonomous underwater vehicle that combines manual control with autonomy, operating in murky water, over uneven terrain and in strong currents at depths from around 2 m to 300 m, producing sharp georeferenced optical models of the seabed.
- **Scout 3** - latest generation, unveiled in 2025, surveying large areas per hour at high resolution from a small deployment footprint.
- **Militarised Scout variant** - a defence-specific version under development for the Royal Netherlands Navy to detect, identify and classify seabed objects, mine-like threats and damage to underwater infrastructure.

## Frameworks, accreditations and memberships

- **Programmes:** NATO DIANA (Defence Innovation Accelerator for the North Atlantic) cohort company; participant in NATO seabed-security experimentation through the Seabed Security Experimentation Centre (SeaSEC).
