# National Composites Centre

> UK national R&D facility providing industry access to composite engineering capability for aerospace, defence and advanced manufacturing sectors.

## What they do

The National Composites Centre (NCC) describes itself as a world-leading innovation organisation that turns research and technology into industrial impact, bridging academia and industry across the product life cycle. It is a not-for-profit centre and one of seven centres in the UK's High Value Manufacturing Catapult, working with organisations from micro-businesses to major primes across aerospace, defence, energy and other sectors, and states it has invested more than £200 million in advanced composites capability.

## Products and solutions

- **Innovation services** - guiding organisations from emerging ideas to real-world application, managing risk and accelerating technology adoption.
- **Technical services** - open-access facilities, equipment and engineering expertise for composite manufacturing development.
- **Supply chain services** - targeted support for SMEs to embed advanced technologies and build business capability.
- **Skills services** - tailored training and workforce development programmes.
- **Manufacturing capability** - braiding, automated fibre placement, high-volume manufacturing, ultra-high-rate deposition, ceramic matrix composites, resin impregnation, and verification and testing facilities.
- **Defence-facing programmes** - the Composites Technology Forum for UK Defence and the Applied Research and Technology Forum for UK Defence, run with defence primes, academia and SMEs, alongside stated work on advanced materials, digital engineering and digital twins.
