# Marlborough Communications

> Supplies advanced RF communications, electronic warfare and uncrewed systems technology to UK defence, security and emergency services customers, drawing on over 40 years in the sector.

## What they do

Marlborough Communications Ltd (MCL) has supplied intelligence collection, communications and robotic technology to the Armed Forces and police since 1980. The company designs and supplies electronic and surveillance technology for RF communications and signals intelligence applications, developing solutions in-house and sourcing complementary products from partners worldwide, and supports equipment through its full service life. MCL organises its offer across four capability areas: Electronic Warfare, Tactical Systems, Uncrewed Systems and Support Services, serving UK MOD, government departments, prime defence contractors, and emergency services including the RNLI.

## Products and solutions

- **Electronic warfare and SIGINT/ELINT systems** - modular, scalable electronic warfare, signals intelligence and electronic intelligence solutions for sea, land and air platforms, described by the company as ultra-lightweight to medium-lightweight and built on open architecture standards and software-definable frameworks.
- **Counter-UAS systems** - library-based and pure-RF detect, disrupt and defeat capabilities for protecting personnel, vehicles and static bases, stated to be in widespread use with UK forces and overseas nations.
- **Tactical dismounted soldier systems** - voice and data-enabled communication systems, hearing protection with situational awareness, walk-on-fit intercom systems and high-performance antennas for military, counter-terrorist and emergency service users.
- **Uncrewed systems** - small UAS and robotic platforms combining sensor payloads, autonomous navigation and integrated secure communications links back to a command centre, including full-motion video ISR payloads and First Person View (FPV) tactical UAS.
- **FalCom tactical audio equipment** - communications equipment supplied in support of the British Army's Dismounted Data System.
