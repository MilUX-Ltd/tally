# Kongsberg Maritime

> Norwegian maritime technology company providing navigation, automation, positioning, propulsion and simulation systems for naval, merchant and offshore customers.

## What they do

Kongsberg Maritime supplies navigation, positioning, automation, propulsion and integrated bridge systems for vessels across naval, merchant marine, offshore and coastal marine markets, alongside K-Sim maritime simulation solutions for crew and dynamic-positioning training. Until 2026 it operated as the Maritime business area of Kongsberg Gruppen; it completed a demerger and began independent trading on Euronext Oslo (ticker KMAR) on 23 April 2026, majority-owned by the Norwegian state. The company's Naval Systems line covers deck machinery, propulsion and manoeuvring equipment, and integration and handling systems for uncrewed underwater and surface vehicles used by more than 70 navies worldwide. Note: Kongsberg's sonar and autonomous underwater vehicle product lines (HUGIN, HISAS, EM-series echosounders) sit under the separate Kongsberg Discovery business following the 2026 group restructuring, and are not part of Kongsberg Maritime.

## Products and solutions

- **K-Sim Navigation** - high-fidelity bridge and navigation simulation platform for training and equipment test and evaluation.
- **K-Sim DP** - dynamic positioning simulator built on the same technology as Kongsberg's K-Pos DP control systems, for DP operator training.
- **K-Pos** - dynamic positioning control system range, IMO DP Equipment Class 1/2/3 compliant, for remote control of propulsion, thrusters and steering gear.
- **K-Bridge / K-Master** - integrated bridge systems and workstations combining navigation, automation and manoeuvring in a single operator environment.
- **K-Chief** - integrated marine automation system for propulsion, power management and auxiliary system monitoring and control.
- **Naval Systems** - deck machinery, propulsion and manoeuvring equipment, and handling systems (including Mission Bay Handling Systems) for deployment and retrieval of uncrewed underwater and surface vehicles on naval platforms.
