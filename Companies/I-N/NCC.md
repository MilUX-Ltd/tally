# NCC

> The National Composites Centre, a High Value Manufacturing Catapult organisation bridging composites research and industrial application across the defence and aerospace sectors.

## What they do

NCC is part of the High Value Manufacturing Catapult and is affiliated with the University of Bristol, having evolved from the National Centre of Excellence in composite technology. Based in Bristol, it translates advanced composites research into industrial impact across the entire product lifecycle, from concept to end-of-life, working with companies of all sizes including defence and aerospace primes and their supply chains. The centre states it has over £250 million invested in its capability and describes itself as a Centre of Excellence for Defence Innovation, supporting UK defence programmes to reduce risk and accelerate the development of advanced materials, including the UK's first carbon fibre development facility.

## Products and solutions

- **Composite manufacturing capability** - automated fibre placement, braiding, ultra-high-rate deposition, ceramic matrix composites and resin impregnation, described as among the world's most advanced automated composites manufacturing facilities.
- **Test and verification** - materials and component testing and verification services supporting the composites product lifecycle.
- **Defence Innovation programme** - dedicated defence-sector engagement supporting MOD and industry partners on advanced materials development.
- **Digital and connectivity infrastructure** - a private industrial 5G network and participation in the UK's first quantum-secure industrial network.
