# NIACE

> Northern Ireland Advanced Composites and Engineering Centre; a technology hub for research and development in advanced materials and engineering, serving aerospace, automotive and defence sectors.

## What they do

NIACE was a purpose-built R&D centre at Belfast International Airport, co-owned and operated by Queen's University Belfast and Ulster University, providing collaborative research and technology development in advanced composites and engineering materials. It opened in 2012 with funding from BIS, Invest Northern Ireland and Bombardier Aerospace, and operated as a shared hub for member companies across aerospace, automotive and defence, offering access to specialist facilities and expertise not typically available to individual SMEs. In October 2021 it was re-launched in partnership with the National Composites Centre, giving members access to the UK-wide High Value Manufacturing Catapult network, with support from Invest Northern Ireland, Spirit AeroSystems and BEIS.

**Note on current status.** The website previously recorded for NIACE (niace-centre.org.uk) no longer resolves to the organisation; it now redirects to unrelated third-party content. NIACE's activities appear to have been superseded by the Advanced Manufacturing Innovation Centre (AMIC), a Belfast Region City Deal project led by Queen's University Belfast, whose "Factory of the Future" facility at Newtownabbey became operational in April 2026. This entry is retained for historical record; the website field has been left as previously recorded rather than substituted, and should be corrected or the page archived once Matt confirms the successor arrangement.

## Products and solutions

- **Composites research and development facilities** - collaborative office space, a composites research laboratory and materials analysis labs for member companies.
- **Capability areas publicly stated** - joining of composites, reusable bagging materials, recycling of high-performance thermoplastics and thermosets, filament winding, sensor integration into composite structures, and low-cost tooling development.
- **Testing and prototyping** - test matrix definition and material/component testing, plus FDM 3D-printing based rapid prototyping and low-use tooling.
