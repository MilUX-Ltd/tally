# NavyX

> The Royal Navy's Autonomy, Lethality and Innovation accelerator, developing and testing autonomous and novel technology for naval operations.

## What they do

NavyX is the Royal Navy's Autonomy, Lethality and Innovation accelerator, established in 2019 to bring new technology into use faster than traditional procurement routes. In April 2025 the Royal Navy created the Disruptive Capabilities and Technologies Office (DCTO), which formally brought NavyX together with the Office of the Chief Technology Officer and the Navy AI Cell into a single organisation; the NavyX name continues to be used publicly for the trials and technology work it originated.

## Products and solutions

- **XV Patrick Blackett** - the Royal Navy's dedicated experimentation vessel, delivered in 2022, used as a modular testbed for uncrewed, AI and sensor trials.
- **MADFOX** - a 13-metre uncrewed surface vessel used in the Royal Navy's first USV trials, including a demonstration launch of a loitering munition under remote control during NATO exercise REPMUS in 2021.
- **APAC-24** - a remotely controlled and autonomous rigid inflatable boat, evaluated by NavyX including live-firing trials.
- **REPMUS participation** - recurring annual participation in NATO's Robotic Experimentation and Prototyping using Maritime Uncrewed Systems exercise in Portugal, trialling uncrewed surface and subsurface systems, anti-submarine warfare capability, and command-and-control equipment.
- **Navigation and sensor trials** - quantum inertial navigation sensor trials with Imperial College London aboard XV Patrick Blackett, and navigation radar trials using Kelvin Hughes and Saab Giraffe systems.
- **Project VANTAGE** - a tri-service programme trialling maritime one-way strike drones, including the first launch of a strike-capable drone from a Royal Navy ship under way, from XV Patrick Blackett in 2026.

## Frameworks, accreditations and memberships

- Delivered through the Royal Navy's relationship with Dstl and the Defence and Security Accelerator (DASA), which fund and support maritime autonomous systems research.
- **Memberships:** ADS Group
