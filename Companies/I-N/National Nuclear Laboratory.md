# National Nuclear Laboratory

> The UK's national laboratory for nuclear science and technology, serving defence, civil nuclear, and homeland security customers. Now publicly styled the United Kingdom National Nuclear Laboratory (UKNNL) following a December 2024 rebrand.

## What they do

National Nuclear Laboratory (NNL), now publicly styled United Kingdom National Nuclear Laboratory (UKNNL), describes its purpose as nuclear science to benefit society. It is the UK's government-owned, operationally independent national laboratory for nuclear fission, covering the complete nuclear fuel cycle from fuel manufacture and power generation through to reprocessing, waste treatment and disposal, and including defence, new nuclear build and homeland security. Its work is organised into four stated focus areas: clean energy, environmental restoration, health and nuclear medicine, and security and non-proliferation.

## Products and solutions

- **Fuel cycle and reactor support** - fuel manufacture, reactor operations support, reprocessing, waste treatment and disposal.
- **Decommissioning and environmental restoration** - support for nuclear site decommissioning programmes, including work at Sellafield.
- **Defence nuclear support** - stated support to the Ministry of Defence on the reactors powering the Royal Navy's submarine fleet, delivered in coordination with Rolls-Royce.
- **Security and non-proliferation** - nuclear site security assessment and design, safeguards support, and delivery of the UK's national support programme to the IAEA.
- **RadBall radiation mapping** - a passive, unpowered 3D radiation-mapping device that collimates radiation onto a radiation-sensitive polymer sphere, later imaged by optical-CT scanning to produce a 3D map of radiation hotspots, used in areas too radioactive or inaccessible for conventional instruments.
- **Central Laboratory and Windscale Laboratory (Sellafield)** - post-irradiation examination and related analytical services supporting plant life extension.

## Frameworks, accreditations and memberships

- **Programmes:** IAEA Collaborating Centre for the Advanced Fuel Cycle (designated 2020); UK SMR consortium
