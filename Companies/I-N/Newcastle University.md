# Newcastle University

> Research-intensive university with defence-relevant expertise in naval architecture, marine engineering, aerospace engineering and intelligent sensing.

## What they do

Newcastle University's School of Engineering covers naval architecture and marine engineering, aerospace engineering, electrical and electronic engineering, and geospatial engineering, among other disciplines. The university runs one of the UK's larger dedicated marine technology research groups, with facilities including a cavitation tunnel and propulsion and hydrodynamics laboratories, and states a "Security and Defence" research theme spanning maritime and offshore engineering, aerospace, sensing and communications, autonomous systems, earth observation and geospatial intelligence, advanced materials, and artificial intelligence. It states it does not support research intended solely for direct offensive military activity, and that its endowment does not invest in armament manufacturers.

## Products and solutions

- **Intelligent Sensing Laboratory** - university research facility for sensing and communications research, opened 2016.
- **EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training in Geospatial Systems** - doctoral training centre run jointly with the University of Nottingham.
- **Academic Centre of Excellence in Cyber Security Research** - designation held since 2013.

## Frameworks, accreditations and memberships

- **Frameworks:** registered supplier on the R-Cloud portal, Accelerated Capability Environment (ACE) and Serapis framework.
- **Memberships:** ADS Group; founding member of the North East Regional Defence and Security Cluster (NERDSC); member of the Research Institute in Science of Cyber Security (RISCS); Armed Forces Covenant signatory.
- **Accreditations:** Naval Architecture and Marine Engineering degree accredited by the Royal Institution of Naval Architects (RINA).
