# DZP Technologies

> Cambridge advanced-materials company developing printable conductive inks and flexible electronics for robotics, sensors, and autonomous systems.

## What they do

DZP Technologies develops water-based conductive inks, graphene dispersions, and electrically conductive adhesives that can be printed or deposited onto flexible and heat-sensitive substrates including textiles, paper, and polymers. The materials underpin flexible and stretchable electronics for wearables, sensors, energy storage, and embedded circuits. In 2025, ARIA selected DZP under its Robot Dexterity programme to develop printed electronics components enabling dexterous robotic manipulation.

## Products and solutions

- **Silver conductive inks** - printable silver formulations for flexible circuit traces and antennas on polymer and textile substrates.
- **Graphene inks and dispersions** - carbon-based conductive materials for supercapacitors, sensors, and 3D-printed electronics.
- **Electrically conductive adhesives** - low-temperature-cure bonding agents for component attachment on flexible assemblies.
- **Thermal management materials** - heat-dissipation pastes for compact embedded electronics.

## Recent news
