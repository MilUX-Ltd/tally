# Destinus UK Limited

> European aerospace and defence company developing hypersonic and supersonic drones, cruise missiles and AI-enabled autonomous systems; UK entity registered in London.

## What they do

Destinus is a European aerospace and defence manufacturer focused on unmanned strike, interceptor and reconnaissance systems across the subsonic to hypersonic speed range. The company was founded in 2021 in Payerne, Switzerland and relocated its headquarters to Amsterdam, Netherlands in late 2024. Destinus UK Limited, incorporated in March 2024 and registered at 21 Holborn Viaduct, London, is the UK entity. The group has raised nearly 400 million euros in equity and debt financing and completed the acquisition of AI avionics specialist Daedalean in January 2026 for approximately 225 million US dollars. Destinus and Rheinmetall announced a joint venture, Rheinmetall Destinus Strike Systems, in April 2026, with Rheinmetall holding 51 per cent and Destinus 49 per cent; the JV intends to manufacture and deliver its first RUTA Block 3 cruise missiles before the end of 2026.

## Products and solutions

- **Ruta (B1/B2/B3)** - cruise missile family; Block 2 has a range up to 450 kilometres and a 250-kilogram payload; Block 3 extends range to 2,000 kilometres powered by the T220 turbojet engine.
- **Hornet Block 2** - dual-role electric interceptor for subsonic threats, with a range of 75 to 150 kilometres; tested by the French Army.
- **Lord** - long-range reconnaissance and strike UAS with a range exceeding 1,000 kilometres.
- **T150 Turbojet Engine** - in-house turbojet powering the Ruta B1 and B2 family; Destinus completed its 1,000th unit in June 2026, reaching industrial-scale production.
- **Daedalean AI Avionics** - AI-enabled flight systems for autonomous and safety-critical aviation applications, acquired January 2026.
