# Dominion Dynamics

> Canadian defence technology startup building an Arctic autonomy and sensing stack for NATO and allied nations.

## What they do

Dominion Dynamics develops an integrated stack combining Arctic-optimised sensing, autonomous systems and networked platforms, describing its mission as providing allied forces with interoperable, rapidly deployable, software-defined systems for extreme and remote environments. The stated goal is to give Canada a domestic defence capability at scale, reducing reliance on large US primes. The company completed field trials of its sensor technology in Northern Ontario and has an active deployment in the Yukon, and its AuraNet platform was used by the Canadian Armed Forces during Operation Nanook-Nunalivut to support mission tracking, planning and real-time communications in the High Arctic.

Dominion Dynamics raised a CAD 21 million seed round led by Georgian, with Bessemer Venture Partners and the British Columbia Investment Management Corporation (BCI) participating, and followed this with a CAD 139 million Series A, reported as the largest Series A raise in Canadian defence technology history.

## Products and solutions

- **AuraNet** - flagship command-and-control software platform that fuses ruggedised Arctic sensor nodes, communications and autonomous systems into a single operating picture.
- **Arctic sensor network** - ruggedised sensor nodes for monitoring Canada's northern frontier, field-tested in Northern Ontario and deployed in the Yukon.
- **Scout** - autonomous collaborative uncrewed aircraft designed to operate alongside crewed fifth-generation fighter aircraft and extend the AuraNet network into austere environments.
