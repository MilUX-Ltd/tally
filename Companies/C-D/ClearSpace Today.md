# ClearSpace Today

> UK arm of a Swiss-founded space group developing in-orbit servicing and active debris removal missions.

## What they do

ClearSpace develops in-orbit servicing and active debris removal missions, covering inspection, life-extension, refuelling, disposal and repair of satellites and the capture and de-orbit of defunct objects. ClearSpace Today Ltd is the UK subsidiary of ClearSpace SA, founded in Switzerland in 2018 and headquartered in Renens; the group also operates in Germany, Luxembourg and the United States. The UK entity leads the UK Space Agency's first active debris removal mission.

## Products and solutions

- **CLEAR** - lead on the UK Space Agency's first active debris removal mission, removing two derelict objects from low-Earth orbit.
- **ClearSpace-1** - ESA-commissioned active debris removal mission targeting the PROBA-1 satellite, expected to launch around 2028.
- **PRELUDE** - ESA in-orbit demonstration mission announced in January 2026, due to launch in 2027.

## Frameworks, accreditations and memberships

- **Memberships:** ADS Group.
- **Programmes:** UK Space Agency (CLEAR); ESA Space Safety Programme (ClearSpace-1, PRELUDE).
