# Conflict Armament Research

> Investigative organisation that documents and traces conventional weapons, ammunition and military materiel found in conflict zones.

## What they do

Conflict Armament Research tracks the supply of conventional weapons, ammunition and related materiel into conflict-affected areas. Field teams document weapons at the point of use, often working with national security and defence forces, then trace items back through the chain of supply to identify how they reached the battlefield. The organisation has documented over 130,000 illicit weapons, munitions and related items across multiple countries since 2014. It has received funding from the European Union and the German, Swiss and UK governments.

## Products and solutions

- **iTrace** - a global weapon reporting system and database combining field investigations with weapon-tracking records; the public reporting interface is free to use.
- **Field investigation and tracing** - in-conflict documentation of weapons and supply-chain analysis for governments and policymakers.
