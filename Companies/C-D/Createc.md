# Createc

> Applied technology SME developing gamma radiation imaging, autonomous robotics, AI, and GNSS-denied sensing systems for nuclear decommissioning, defence, and security markets.

## What they do

Createc designs and deploys advanced sensing, robotics, and imaging systems, with particular depth in gamma radiation detection and 3D mapping, GNSS-denied navigation, LiDAR, and autonomous robotic platforms. Founded in 2010 in Cockermouth, Cumbria, the company employs more than 60 people including 13 PhDs across three specialist laboratories, with additional offices in Oxford and Oslo. Its technology stack is explicitly dual-use: the same robotics, sensing, and AI systems underpin nuclear decommissioning work for Sellafield, Dounreay, and TEPCO Fukushima, and defence and security applications including the UK MOD's SAPIENT autonomous sensor architecture.

## Products and solutions

- **N-Visage** - flagship gamma radiation 3D imaging system; 360 x 180 degree field, CZT detector, energy range 30 keV to 2 MeV, up to 1 Sv/hr dose tolerance; deployable through 90mm ports.
- **N-Visage Fusion** - 3D radiation field modelling and visualisation software.
- **N-Visage Explore** - robotic payload variant for real-time source discovery on mobile autonomous platforms.
- **SLATE (Scanning Laser Automatic Threat Extraction)** - sensor module developed for Dstl's SAPIENT programme; provides autonomous threat detection for perimeter security and counter-UAS applications.
- **RISER UAV** - GPS-denied autonomous drone (co-developed with Blue Bear Systems Research) with integrated N-Visage radiation sensing; deployed inside Fukushima Daiichi reactor buildings.
- **Flyt** - nuclear decommissioning and waste package management software.
- **Station Guardian / Situate** - AI and LiDAR-based crowd monitoring platform; permanently deployed at London Waterloo station from 2024 following Dstl-funded development through SAPIENT and DASA competitions.
