# Ploughshare
> MOD-owned commercialisation vehicle that turns crown-owned intellectual property from DSTL, AWE and other government labs into licensed products and spinouts.

## What they do
Ploughshare is wholly owned by the UK Ministry of Defence and operates as the commercial licensing arm for government-developed IP. It negotiates licence agreements and creates spinout companies across four portfolios: defence and security, digital science, health and wellbeing, and climate and environment. The organisation works directly with R&D teams inside DSTL, AWE and other MOD science and technology establishments. Established in 2005, Ploughshare has exclusive access to MOD defence intellectual property and has commercialised over 140 technologies, creating more than 15 spinout companies to date.

## Products and solutions

Ploughshare does not develop technology itself; it licenses and spins out MOD and Dstl-originated IP. Named examples from its own published portfolio and licensee pages include:

- **CrystalCast (via licensee Riskaware)** - a Dstl-developed decision-support modelling tool that visualises complex data at speed, used for "what-if" scenario planning; originally developed for epidemic and pandemic modelling and licensed to Riskaware for commercial availability.
- **HASP Suite (via licensee Riskaware)** - Hazard Assessment, Simulation and Prediction software toolset providing situational awareness and decision support in the CBRN/hazardous materials domain.
- **Cyber Griffin** - a licensed cyber protection capability, used by the City of London Police to provide free cyber protection support to SMEs and charities.
- **P2i Ltd** - the first Dstl spin-out (2005), commercialising liquid-repellent nanocoating technology.
- **Claresys Ltd** - spin-out established in 2008 to exploit patented Dstl technology.
- **Energetics Technology Ltd** - licensee specialising in energetic materials, blast mitigation and protection.
