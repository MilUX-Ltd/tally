# RTX

> One of the world's largest aerospace and defence companies, and parent of Collins Aerospace, Pratt & Whitney and Raytheon.

## What they do

RTX employs over 185,000 people globally and reported revenues exceeding $80 billion in 2024. Through its three principal subsidiaries it covers commercial and military aviation systems, propulsion, integrated defence systems, and advanced technology solutions. The company has a UK presence including government relations activity in London.

## Products and solutions

- **Collins Aerospace** - Aerospace systems, avionics, aerostructures and mission systems, including the ARC-210 airborne radio and TruNet family of tactical networked communications products, and PNT products such as MAPS Gen II and NavHub-100.
- **Pratt & Whitney** - Aircraft propulsion for commercial and military platforms.
- **Raytheon** - Defence electronics, missiles and integrated air and missile defence systems, including the AN/TPY-2 and Global Patriot radars, the AN/AQS-20C mine-hunting sonar, and multi-spectral EO/IR targeting systems such as MTS.
