# Royal Air Force

> The United Kingdom's air and space force, responsible for air defence, strike, intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance.

## What they do

The Royal Air Force is one of the three Armed Services of the United Kingdom, operating under the Ministry of Defence. It delivers air superiority, strike, close air support, air transport, intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance, and space capabilities. It is an active procurer of defence technology across platforms, sensors, software, and services.

## Products and solutions

- **Autonomous Collaborative Platforms (ACP)** - a published RAF strategy for introducing uncrewed and autonomous aircraft alongside crewed platforms, with remote mission operators commanding and supervising multiple autonomous vehicles within each ACP system.
- **StormShroud** - an uncrewed aircraft brought into RAF service as the first platform in the emerging Autonomous Collaborative Platforms family, intended for electronic warfare and support roles.
- **Protector RG Mk1 (MQ-9B)** - remotely piloted air system providing persistent intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance and strike capability.
- **RAF Regiment Counter-UAS** - ground-based force protection element responsible for detecting and defeating hostile uncrewed aerial systems around RAF stations and deployed locations.
- **Rapid Capabilities Office (RCO)** - RAF unit working with industry partners, Defence Equipment & Support and the Defence Science and Technology Laboratory to accelerate autonomous and uncrewed platform development into service.
