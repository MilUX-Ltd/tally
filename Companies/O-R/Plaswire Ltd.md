# Plaswire Ltd

> Northern Ireland manufacturer processing co-polymers for aircraft interiors and developing circular-economy solutions for difficult-to-recycle aerospace polymers.

## What they do

Plaswire processes co-polymers used in aircraft interior applications and works on developing new processes for difficult-to-recycle polymers. The company provides collection services, secure material destruction, and returns recovered materials to the supply chain within a cradle-to-cradle model. Its stated aim is to repurpose 100% of end-of-life aircraft materials, including interior polymers and airframe carbon fibre, into a sustainable replacement product for wood, concrete and virgin plastics, reducing the carbon footprint of aircraft manufacture, refurbishment and decommissioning.

## Products and solutions

- **RX Polymer** - proprietary recycled polymer made from difficult-to-recycle waste, including aircraft interior polymers, airframe composites, carbon fibre, and wind turbine blades, processed through shredding, granulation, agglomeration, washing and extrusion. Used as a substitute for precast concrete, virgin plastic and wood-based construction products.
- **Aviation materials repurposing** - collection and processing service for end-of-life aircraft interior and composite materials, converting them into RX Polymer rather than incineration or landfill.
- **Blockchain traceability** - end-to-end traceability system, developed with digital trade specialist ubloquity, tracking recycled polymer from collection through to reuse; first applied to wind turbine blade recycling with stated potential to extend into aerospace.
