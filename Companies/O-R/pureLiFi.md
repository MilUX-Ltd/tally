# pureLiFi

> Edinburgh-based developer of LiFi optical wireless communications technology, with a strong defence and national-security line.

## What they do

pureLiFi develops LiFi (light fidelity), high-speed, bidirectional, networked and mobile wireless communication that uses the light spectrum rather than radio frequency to transmit data. It builds the optoelectronic components and systems that make LiFi work, for uses ranging from defence and government to enterprise. Because LiFi uses light rather than RF, the company markets it as inherently more secure, since the signal cannot be detected, intercepted or jammed outside its defined cone of light coverage. It was founded in 2012 as a University of Edinburgh spin-out (the legal entity was incorporated in 2011).

## Products and solutions

- **Kitefin** - LiFi defence system, an optical wireless communication system for secure data transmission without radio frequencies, deployed with the US Army in Tactical and Office variants.
- **Kitefin XE** - next-generation LiFi system launched June 2025, offering room-filling coverage over 80 square metres at gigabit capacity and compliant with the IEEE 802.11bb standard.
- **Light Antenna ONE / Light Antenna Module** - optoelectronic component enabling OEMs to integrate LiFi into connected devices, compliant with IEEE 802.11bb.
- **Through-window bridging technology** - proprietary LiFi technique to pass high-speed data through a window, the basis of a fixed-wireless-access partnership.

## Frameworks, accreditations and memberships
