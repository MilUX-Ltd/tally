# Queen's University Belfast

> Research-intensive university with active aerospace, defence and advanced manufacturing research activity, including a UK Innovation and Knowledge Centre for cyber security.

## What they do

Queen's University Belfast is a broadly based, research-driven university with strong interdisciplinary research spanning engineering, aerospace, materials, and advanced manufacturing. It operates through research clusters and interdisciplinary forums, with priority placed on nationally and internationally recognised research excellence. Its Centre for Secure Information Technologies (CSIT) is the UK's Innovation and Knowledge Centre for cyber security, combining research with commercialisation and industry collaboration.

## Products and solutions

- **CSIT (Centre for Secure Information Technologies)** - UK Innovation and Knowledge Centre for cyber security, founded 2009, with research spanning post-quantum and advanced cryptography, network security, secure software systems, trusted hardware and data privacy; an Academic Centre of Excellence in Cyber Security Research.
- **Cyber-AI Hub** - launched 2023 within CSIT to research the intersection of cyber security and AI, including AI-driven threat detection, robustness of AI-based malware detection against adversarial attacks, and formal verification of AI models deployed on autonomous systems.
- **Rolls-Royce Cybersecurity Technology Research Network** - CSIT is one of three university research centres worldwide in this network, alongside CERIAS (Purdue) and CyLab (Carnegie Mellon).
- **MSc Applied Cyber Security** - postgraduate taught programme, including a variant with a professional internship year.
