# Oxford Quantum Circuits Ltd

> UK quantum computing company delivering quantum-computing-as-a-service via superconducting circuits.

## What they do

Oxford Quantum Circuits (OQC) is Europe's first quantum-computing-as-a-service (QCaaS) provider and the only company deploying quantum systems into commercial data centres. Its core technology uses a patented 3-D superconducting circuit architecture, branded Dimon, designed to scale whilst maintaining speed, with a sovereign and secure-by-design offering. The company states it works with defence primes and with defence and security organisations to deliver proof of concepts and experimentation activity, and describes itself as the only company integrating quantum into secure data centre infrastructure.

## Products and solutions

- **Quantum-computing-as-a-service (QCaaS)** - cloud access to OQC's superconducting quantum processors, deployed inside partner data centres.
- **On-premise quantum deployment** - a quantum processing unit sold and deployed directly on a customer's premises, for example at the CESGA high-performance computing centre in Spain, running quantum and classical compute side by side.
- **Quantum-AI data centre integration** - joint deployments with data-centre partners combining quantum processors with AI/HPC infrastructure, including a facility in New York built with NVIDIA GH200 Grace Hopper Superchips.
