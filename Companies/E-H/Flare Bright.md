# Flare Bright

> Machine learning and digital twin software for GNSS-denied drone navigation, testing, and evaluation.

## What they do

Flare Bright develops machine learning software and digital twin technology for unmanned aerial and undersea vehicles. Founded in November 2015 and based at Westcott Venture Park in Buckinghamshire, the company builds software that enables drones to fly accurately in GNSS-denied environments, perform in-flight wind sensing, and support regulatory safety case assurance. Flare Bright has shifted from developing its own drone hardware to providing software that integrates with existing and future platforms built by defence primes and other manufacturers. It holds contracts with the UK MOD, Dstl, and the US Department of Defense.

## Products and solutions

- **Digital twin platform** - software environment that models unmanned aircraft and applies machine learning to solve navigation, control, sensing, and optimisation problems, enabling GNSS-denied flight.
- **Extera** - containment assurance software for critical drone operations, designed to accelerate testing and evaluation programmes for novel drone systems; introduced at DSEI 2025.
