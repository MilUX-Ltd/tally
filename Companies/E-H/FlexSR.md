# FlexSR

> University of Oxford spinout developing flexible, small-model speech recognition for defence, security and policing applications.

## What they do

FlexSR Limited is a private company incorporated in October 2023, a spinout from the University of Oxford's Language and Brain Laboratory. Its speech recognition approach is built on a linguistic model of phonological features rather than the acoustic or large-language-model methods used by conventional automatic speech recognition (ASR), so it can recognise words and phrases across languages, accents and pronunciations without model training. The company states this lightweight approach suits real-time, low-compute deployment on edge devices, wearables, body-worn cameras or radios, including hostile or hands-free operational environments where conventional interfaces are impractical.

## Products and solutions

- **FlexSR speech recognition engine** - flexible ASR technology matching speech directly to phonological features and a defined lexicon, without acoustic model training, licensed for integration into third-party products and platforms.
- **Real-time transcription quality checker** - error-detection capability for AI-generated call transcripts and summaries, piloted with a UK police service to improve confidence in AI call-handling pipelines.
- **Hands-free trigger-word monitoring** - real-time speech monitoring for front-line police and security personnel to initiate assistance broadcasts when hands are not free to operate a device.
- **Surveillance and word-spotting** - recognition of target words and phrases directly from audio across multiple languages and accents without building per-language or per-accent models.

## Frameworks, accreditations and memberships
