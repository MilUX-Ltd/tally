# Element Materials Technology

> The world's largest independent provider of testing, inspection, and certification services to the aerospace and defence sector, headquartered in London and owned by Temasek (Singapore).

## What they do

Element Materials Technology provides testing, inspection, and certification services across aerospace, defence, and advanced industrial supply chains. Their defence capability spans materials testing, product qualification testing, non-destructive testing, environmental and EMC testing, vibration and shock testing, calibration, and space simulation testing. The company operates over 270 laboratory locations worldwide and has supported programmes including F-35, F-22, A400M, CH-53K, Challenger MBT, Mastiff, and Husky.

UK laboratory locations with defence relevance include Hitchin, Hull, Malvern, Skelmersdale, Warwick, and Wimborne. The company also operates Element Defense (elementdefense.com) as a dedicated US Space and Defense division.

## Products and solutions

- **Materials testing** - destructive and non-destructive testing of metals, composites, coatings, and structural components to aerospace and defence standards.
- **Product qualification testing (PQT)** - environmental, EMC, vibration, shock, and climatic testing to military specifications across six UK sites.
- **Space simulation testing** - thermal-vacuum, vibration, and acoustic testing for space programmes.
- **Calibration services** - traceable calibration across mechanical, electrical, and dimensional measurement disciplines.

## Frameworks, accreditations and memberships

- **Frameworks:** JOSCAR (UK Product Qualification Testing laboratories; recognised by BAE Systems, Babcock, QinetiQ, NATS, Leonardo, Meggitt, and the MOD)
- **Accreditations:** ISO 9001:2015 (multiple UK sites)
- **Memberships:** ADS Group

## Ownership note

Element Materials Technology is operationally headquartered in London. Ultimate ownership is Temasek, the Singaporean state investment fund, which acquired the business from Bridgepoint in July 2022 for approximately $7 billion. The company is a substantive UK-headquartered business, not a thin UK entity of a foreign firm.
