# Greenjets

> UK manufacturer of electric ducted-fan propulsion systems for drones, air taxis and electric aircraft, spun out from Blue Bear Systems Research.

## What they do

Greenjets designs and manufactures electric ducted-fan propulsion systems for advanced air mobility, with a product range spanning 2 kW to 1 MW output. Its ducted-fan architecture is stated to be quieter, safer and more efficient than an equivalent open-bladed propeller, with the company citing up to 93 per cent noise reduction, up to 45 per cent lower power consumption for a given thrust, and flight speeds of up to 125 m/s. The company began as Project InCEPTion, a research programme within the ePropulsion division of Blue Bear Systems Research, and span out as an independent company at the end of 2022. Its team draws on aerospace and motorsport experience from Rolls-Royce, BAE Systems and Formula 1. Greenjets states it supplies propulsion systems to airframe manufacturers across North America and Europe, and offers an airframe integration service alongside its engines.

## Products and solutions

- **Sycamore 160** - compact ducted-fan engine for fixed-wing UAVs under 25 kg; 6 kg maximum static thrust at 792 g all-up weight, designed for a 26 m/s cruise speed with a stated 75 per cent noise reduction.
- **Aspen 212** - higher-output engine in the Aspen family for larger UAV and advanced air mobility platforms.
- **Hemlock** - compact, high-thrust engine for high flight-speed applications.
- **Integrated Power Advantage** - airframe integration service pairing Greenjets propulsion systems with customer aircraft design.
