# Fife College

> Scottish further and higher education college delivering defence workforce development and advanced manufacturing training, with a campus embedded in Babcock's Rosyth Dockyard.

## What they do

Fife College is a Scottish further and higher education institution offering over 400 courses across engineering, construction, digital technologies, health and social care, business, and creative industries. It serves more than 20,000 students annually and runs over 420 employer partnerships. The college's Rosyth Engineering Campus sits inside Babcock International's Rosyth Dockyard on the Firth of Forth, with approximately 10,000 sq m of purpose-built training facilities focused on engineering, renewables, digital, and advanced manufacturing. The college is one of six partners in the Arrol-Gibb Innovation Campus (AGIC) at Rosyth, alongside the Universities of Edinburgh and Strathclyde, Scottish Enterprise, Skills Development Scotland, and Fife Council.

## Products and solutions

- **Defence workforce development** - bespoke programmes supplying upskilled STEM workers for the Type 31 frigate programme and other national and international defence programmes at Rosyth; includes Production Support Operative (PSO) and Trainee Welder pathways delivered under the Babcock MoU.
- **Advanced manufacturing and engineering training** - apprenticeships and programmes covering composites, robotics, nuclear manufacturing, and digital shipbuilding at the Rosyth campus.
- **Arrol-Gibb Innovation Campus** - collaborative research and training campus at Rosyth supporting the Type 31 build across advanced manufacturing, digital shipbuilding, and related disciplines.
