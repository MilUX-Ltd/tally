# Eutelsat OneWeb

> Integrated GEO-LEO satellite operator providing government and defence connectivity across land, maritime, and air domains.

## What they do

Eutelsat OneWeb was formed in September 2023 through the merger of Eutelsat Communications and OneWeb, combining Eutelsat's 36 geostationary (GEO) satellites with OneWeb's 652-satellite low-earth-orbit (LEO) constellation operating at approximately 1,200 km altitude. The company provides hybrid multi-orbit satellite broadband connectivity across commercial, government, and defence verticals. The UK Government holds a golden share and a board seat in the company. The parent group, Eutelsat Communications SA, is headquartered in Paris; the UK operational centre is at West Works Building, 195 Wood Lane, London.

## Products and solutions

- **OneWeb LEO constellation** - 652-satellite LEO network providing low-latency broadband at global coverage, used for government and military connectivity including Comms-on-the-Move applications.
- **Intellian OW7MP manpack terminal** - the first military-grade LEO manpack terminal for the OneWeb network, co-developed with Intellian; fits a standard military rucksack, supports Comms-on-the-Move (5-130 kph) and Comms-on-the-Pause, and operates in GPS-denied environments with throughput up to 57 x 10 Mbps.
- **Kymeta Goshawk u8 flat-panel terminal** - multi-orbit terminal available on the Eutelsat OneWeb LEO network for government and military customers.
- **Satellite Virtual Network Operator (SVNO)** - digital product giving government and defence customers full control over SATCOM service provisioning.
- **Hybrid multi-orbit SATCOM** - combined GEO high-throughput and LEO low-latency configuration supporting ISR missions across manned and unmanned aircraft, land, and maritime platforms.
